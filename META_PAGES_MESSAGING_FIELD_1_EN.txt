**1. How the app uses this permission:**

Our app (AISP Service) is an automated messaging management tool for Instagram Business accounts. The pages_messaging permission is used exclusively for the following purposes:

(1) Real-time Instagram Direct Messages Reception
- The app subscribes to page webhooks to receive Instagram Direct Messages sent to the connected Instagram Business account in real-time
- Webhook endpoint: https://{backend-url}/instagram/webhook
- This enables immediate processing of incoming messages and triggering automated responses

(2) Customer Support Message Sending
- The app allows page managers to send customer support messages through the Instagram Messaging API
- Response messages are sent in reply to customer inquiries received via Instagram Direct Messages
- All messages are sent on behalf of the page manager who has authorized the app

(3) Automated Response System
- When a customer sends a message to the Instagram Business account, the app can automatically respond based on predefined rules or AI-powered responses
- This enables page managers to provide timely customer support even outside business hours

**2. Value provided to people using the app:**

The pages_messaging permission provides the following value to page managers:

- **Never Miss Customer Messages**: Receive real-time notifications when customers send messages to their Instagram Business account
- **Automated Customer Support**: Automatically respond to common inquiries, reducing response time and improving customer satisfaction
- **Centralized Message Management**: Manage all Instagram Direct Messages from a single dashboard, making it easier to track and respond to customer inquiries
- **24/7 Availability**: Provide customer support even when page managers are not actively monitoring their Instagram account
- **Improved Response Efficiency**: Quickly identify and prioritize urgent messages through automated message routing and categorization

**3. Why this permission is needed for the app's functionality:**

The pages_messaging permission is **essential** for our app's core functionality:

(1) Webhook Subscription Requirement
- To receive Instagram Direct Messages in real-time, the app must subscribe to page webhooks with the "messages" field
- Meta's webhook API requires pages_messaging permission to subscribe to message events
- Without this permission, the app cannot receive incoming messages and cannot function as an automated messaging tool

(2) Message Sending Capability
- The app needs to send customer support messages in response to incoming inquiries
- The Instagram Messaging API requires pages_messaging permission to send messages on behalf of the page
- This is a core feature that page managers rely on for customer communication

(3) Complete Messaging Workflow
- The app provides a complete messaging workflow: receive messages → process/analyze → send responses
- Both receiving and sending capabilities are required for the app to function as intended
- Without pages_messaging, the app would only be able to read messages but not respond, making it incomplete for customer support use cases

**4. Data Usage Compliance:**

All data received through the pages_messaging permission is used in accordance with Meta's allowed use cases:

- **Customer Support Only**: All messages are used solely for customer support and communication purposes
- **No Unauthorized Data Sharing**: Message content and customer data are never shared with third parties without explicit consent
- **Aggregated Analytics**: We may use aggregated, anonymized, and non-identifiable message data (that cannot be re-identified) to improve our app's response accuracy and operational efficiency
- **Data Security**: All message data is encrypted in transit and at rest, following industry best practices
- **User Control**: Page managers have full control over their message data and can revoke access at any time
